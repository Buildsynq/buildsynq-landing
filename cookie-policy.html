Cookie Policy

How BuildSynq uses cookies and similar tracking technologies

Last Updated: February 24, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how BuildSynq, Inc. uses cookies and similar technologies on our website and platform. By continuing to use our Services, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They allow websites to recognize your device, remember your preferences, and improve your experience. Similar technologies include web beacons, pixels, local storage, and session storage.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the platform to function. They cannot be disabled. They include:

Cookie Purpose Duration
session_token Maintains your authenticated session Session
csrf_token Prevents cross-site request forgery attacks Session
auth_refresh Keeps you logged in between visits 30 days

Functional Cookies

These cookies remember your preferences and settings:

Cookie Purpose Duration
user_preferences Stores dashboard layout and display preferences 1 year
timezone Remembers your local timezone for accurate timestamps 1 year
lang_pref Stores your language preference 1 year

Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how users interact with our platform so we can improve it:

Cookie / Technology Provider Purpose Duration
_ga, _gid Google Analytics Page views, session duration, feature usage 2 years / 24 hrs
usage_analytics BuildSynq (internal) Feature adoption and error tracking 90 days

Performance Cookies

These cookies help us diagnose technical issues and monitor platform performance:

Cookie Purpose Duration
perf_monitor Measures page load times and API response times Session
error_trace Captures error context for debugging 7 days

3. Third-Party Cookies

Some third-party services we use may set their own cookies:

  • Stripe: Sets cookies for fraud detection and payment security. See Stripe's Privacy Policy.
  • Calendly: Sets cookies when you schedule a demo. See Calendly's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Fonts: May set cookies when loading typography assets.

4. Cookie Banner and Consent

When you first visit BuildSynq, you will see a cookie consent banner. You can choose to accept all cookies, accept only necessary cookies, or customize your preferences. Strictly necessary cookies cannot be disabled as they are required for the platform to operate.

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You can manage cookies in several ways:

  • Platform settings: Update your cookie preferences at any time in your account settings under Privacy.
  • Browser settings: Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. Note that blocking necessary cookies may prevent the platform from functioning correctly.
  • Browser links: Chrome | Firefox | Safari | Edge
  • Opt-out of Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-out

6. Do Not Track

Some browsers send "Do Not Track" signals. BuildSynq currently does not alter its data collection practices in response to Do Not Track signals, though we continue to monitor industry standards in this area.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. We will notify you of material changes via the platform or email.
